=== Kiroku Shibuya Adapter Overhead Benchmark ===
    5 iterations per measurement
    GHC 9.12.2, -O2, -threaded -rtsopts "-with-rtsopts=-N -A32m"
    macOS (Apple Silicon), ephemeral PostgreSQL

--- 100 events ---

  bare subscribe:      median 3 ms  [3 ms .. 4 ms]  (31566 events/s, 32 μs/event)
  subscriptionStream:  median 2 ms  [2 ms .. 3 ms]  (42052 events/s, 24 μs/event)
  shibuya adapter:     median 1 ms  [1 ms .. 1 ms]  (75930 events/s, 13 μs/event)

  stream vs bare:    -25%
  shibuya vs bare:   -58%
  shibuya vs stream: -45%  (adapter + framework overhead)

--- 1000 events ---

  bare subscribe:      median 10 ms  [10 ms .. 11 ms]  (96256 events/s, 10 μs/event)
  subscriptionStream:  median 10 ms  [10 ms .. 11 ms]  (95776 events/s, 10 μs/event)
  shibuya adapter:     median 11 ms  [10 ms .. 11 ms]  (91609 events/s, 11 μs/event)

  stream vs bare:    +1%
  shibuya vs bare:   +5%
  shibuya vs stream: +5%  (adapter + framework overhead)

--- 5000 events ---

  bare subscribe:      median 44 ms  [42 ms .. 46 ms]  (113196 events/s, 9 μs/event)
  subscriptionStream:  median 48 ms  [43 ms .. 49 ms]  (104323 events/s, 10 μs/event)
  shibuya adapter:     median 47 ms  [46 ms .. 47 ms]  (107386 events/s, 9 μs/event)

  stream vs bare:    +9%
  shibuya vs bare:   +5%
  shibuya vs stream: -3%  (adapter + framework overhead)

--- Analysis ---

At 100 events, startup costs dominate — the stream-based approaches appear
faster because the TBQueue decouples the subscription worker from the
consumer, allowing the worker to batch-fetch ahead while the consumer
processes.

At 1000 events, the approaches converge. The Shibuya framework adds ~5%
overhead over bare subscribe, which includes: TBQueue bridge, morphInner
monad lifting, Ingested/Envelope wrapping, NQE supervision, metrics
tracking, and ack handling.

At 5000 events, all three approaches are within noise of each other
(~100K–113K events/s). The database catch-up query dominates the total
time, making adapter overhead negligible in practice.

The Shibuya adapter's overhead (supervision, metrics, ack semantics) is
effectively free for real-world event store workloads where PostgreSQL
I/O is the bottleneck.
